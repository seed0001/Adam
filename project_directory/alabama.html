About Alabama

Alabama, located in the southeastern region of the United States, has a rich and diverse history. Originally inhabited by various indigenous tribes, the area was explored by Spanish explorers in the 16th century. Over the centuries, Alabama has played a significant role in American history, from its involvement in the Civil War to its pivotal role in the Civil Rights Movement.

In the 19th century, Alabama's economy was largely based on agriculture, particularly cotton, which was heavily reliant on slave labor. The state seceded from the Union in 1861, joining the Confederate States of America. After the Civil War, Alabama faced a long and difficult period of reconstruction. In the 20th century, Alabama became a focal point for the Civil Rights Movement, with events such as the Montgomery Bus Boycott and the Selma to Montgomery marches highlighting the struggle for racial equality.
